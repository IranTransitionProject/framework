## B1) Nuclear Program Status (Post-Midnight Hammer)
* **Background:** US bombed Fordow, Natanz, and Isfahan in June 2025 ("Operation Midnight Hammer," 12-day war). Ceasefire held since. [Fact]
* **Reconstruction:** Iran rebuilding at Natanz, Fordow, Isfahan. Satellite imagery (Feb 2026) shows roofs built over damaged buildings at Isfahan and Natanz to block satellite observation. All three tunnel entrances at Isfahan now buried under soil — mirrors defensive measures taken before Midnight Hammer. [Fact — satellite imagery, ISIS reports]
* **Pickaxe Mountain:** Clandestine facility ~1 mile south of Natanz. Security wall built around perimeter, tunnel networks expanded. Feb 2026 imagery shows active hardening of tunnel entrances against future strikes. [Fact — satellite imagery]
* **HEU inventory:** ~440 kg of 60% enriched uranium referenced in diplomatic discussions. IAEA confirms material has not been verified as moved from original sites. Some or all may be buried under rubble. [Fact — IAEA DG Grossi, Feb 2026 Munich]
* **Total stockpile:** 9,247 kg enriched to various levels up to 60%, per Feb 2025 IAEA report. [Fact — IAEA]
* **IAEA access:** Denied since strikes. Grossi stated (Feb 2026): "If the program were being rebuilt right now, would you know? No, because we are not getting access to these places." [Fact — Foreign Policy interview, Feb 18, 2026]
* **Breakout timeline (UPDATED):**
    * **Pre-Midnight Hammer:** Less than 1 week for enough weapons-grade HEU for one weapon (DIA, May 2025). IAEA assessed enough material for 9 weapons with further enrichment. [Fact — High]
    * **Post-strike (Jul 2025):** 1–3 months estimated (intelligence assessments). [Inference — Med]
    * **Current trajectory:** Continued reconstruction could return breakout windows to 1–2 weeks by mid-2026, per National Interest analysis (Feb 2026). [Inference — Med, single-source]
    * **Technical restoration:** 12–18 months to restore significant enrichment capacity if reconstruction continues. [Inference — Med]
    * **Weaponization gap:** IAEA reports Iran does not yet have a verified viable weapon design or suitable detonation system. Weaponization would take additional months to years. Knowledge cannot be unlearned. [Fact — High]
    * **Key uncertainty:** Without IAEA inspector access, all current assessments rely on satellite imagery and SIGINT — inadequate for determining breakout timelines inside hardened underground facilities. [Fact — High]

## B2) Missile Inventory
* **Pre-war arsenal:** Estimated ~2,500 ballistic missiles. Strategic goal was 8,000 for saturation capability against Israeli defenses. [Fact/Inference — Med, Alma Center]
* **War damage:** Israeli strikes destroyed ~two-thirds of ballistic missile launchers and between one-third to one-half of the missile arsenal. [Inference — Med, Israeli assessments]
* **Current inventory:** Shrunk from ~2,500 pre-war. Iran attempting to rebuild toward 2,000 missile stock. [Inference — Med, Alma Center Feb 2026]
* **Solid fuel crisis:** Medium-range ballistic missile array experienced critical attrition. Underground infrastructure survived and permits reconstruction if Iran obtains missing raw materials. Solid-fuel production capacity is the bottleneck. [Inference — Med]
* **Khorramshahr-4:** Long-range ballistic missile deployed in underground facilities. [Fact]
* **Reconstruction:** Missile complexes rebuilt at Tehran, Kermanshah, Semnan, Gulf coast. [Fact — satellite imagery, Times of Israel]
* **Ilam Province launch:** Iran reportedly conducted a missile launch near western border with Iraq, Dec 30, 2025. [Fact — Iranian media, unverified purpose]
* **Red line:** Missiles declared "nonnegotiable" — Shamkhani (Feb 12), Takht-Ravanchi (Feb 15). [Fact]
* **Chinese route:** Reports indicate Iran seeking to obtain missing missile raw materials via Chinese supply chains. [Inference — Low, Alma Center]
* **FM Araghchi claim:** Stated Iran has "reconstructed everything that was damaged" — likely overstated for negotiating leverage. [Fact — statement; Inference — assessment]

## B6) IRGC Internal Dynamics Post-War — PRIORITY 1 GAP, PARTIALLY FILLED

* **New IRGC commander:** Mohammad Pakpour appointed as Salami's successor by Khamenei. [Fact]
* **Vahidi elevation:** Ahmad Vahidi appointed IRGC deputy commander Dec 27, 2025. Former Quds Force commander (1988–98), linked to 1994 AMIA bombing (INTERPOL red notice), close to Mojtaba Khamenei. [Fact]
* **Naqdi removal:** Mohammad Reza Naqdi quietly removed from a command position. Delayed acknowledgment, conflicting references, and absence of formal handover suggest internal sensitivities. [Fact — NCRI, Oct 2025]
* **Factional structure:** Analysts describe IRGC as "not monolithic conservative bloc but rather diverse power centers with competing interests." Hardline factions advocate maximum repression; pragmatic elements may recognize prolonged instability threatens IRGC's own economic assets. [Inference — Med]

* **Provincial rotation:** Reports that Tehran rotated more loyal IRGC contingents from other provinces to replace units showing signs of dissent — indicating localized moral and psychological strain on some Basij and provincial IRGC units. [Inference — Med, Stimson Center]

Khamenei's difficulty cohering this generation (evidenced by Vahidi's rapid reassignment) is a structural vulnerability that would amplify during a succession crisis. The institution's willingness to commit mass violence in Jan 2026 demonstrates organizational capacity but may mask internal fractures not yet visible externally. [Inference — Med]

## B8) IRGC Naval & Strait of Hormuz Posture — NEW, Feb 2026
### B8.1) "Smart Control of the Strait of Hormuz" Exercise (Feb 16–18, 2026)
* **Exercise name:** "Smart Control of the Strait of Hormuz." Conducted by IRGC Navy in the Strait of Hormuz, Persian Gulf, and Gulf of Oman. Began Feb 16. [Fact — FARS, Tasnim, multiple]
* **Live-fire components:** Anti-ship cruise missiles fired from Iranian coast and islands at targets in the Strait. IRGC naval drone units and submarine units operated from three Iranian islands (Abu Musa, Greater Tunb, Lesser Tunb). [Fact — Iranian media, TWZ]
* **Armed drones:** FARS News described drones "capable of engaging both air and sea targets" as "newest strategic platforms" deployed "in significant numbers" — names and specs classified. [Fact — FARS; capability claims unverified]
* **Strait closure (UNPRECEDENTED):** Iran announced temporary closure of parts of the Strait of Hormuz "for a few hours" on Feb 17 for "safety and maritime concerns." This is the first time Iran has announced closure of the Strait since Trump threatened military action in January 2026, and possibly the first announced closure since the Iran-Iraq War (1980s mining). [Fact — PBS, AP, The National, Washington Times]
* **Operational messaging:** IRGC Navy commander: "The weapon that reaches the battlefield on the day of war is different from what is shown in the exercise." This signals the exercise demonstrates minimum, not maximum, capability. [Fact — FARS via The National]
* **Second exercise in weeks:** This was the second live-fire exercise in the Strait in recent weeks. The first (late Jan/early Feb) did not include announced closures — the escalation was in the closure announcement itself. [Fact — AP]


### B8.2) Strategic Context
* **Hormuz chokepoint:** ~21 nautical miles at narrowest. 20–30% of global daily oil consumption (~20M+ barrels/day) transits the waterway. No alternative route exists. [Fact — High]
* **Khamenei statement (Feb 17):** "A warship is a dangerous apparatus, but more dangerous than the warship is the weapon that can sink the warship." Also: "The strongest military in the world might sometimes receive a blow so hard that it can't stand up again." [Fact — Iranian state TV, Washington Times]
* **Historical precedent:** INSS Iran expert Danny Citrinowicz assessed that Iran last closed the Strait during the Iran-Iraq war when it mined the waterway — calling this a message that any strike on Iran would have global economic impact. [Fact — PBS/AP]
* **Mine warfare capability:** Iran retains naval mine warfare capacity. Mine countermeasure assets in the region are limited. Even a credible indication of mining activity can impose lengthy countermeasure operations and produce immediate effects on maritime traffic. Mines are a low-cost, high-disruption tool. [Fact/Inference — Med, Army Recognition]
* **Coastal defense inventory:** C-701 Kosar, C-704 Nasr, C-802 Noor, C-802A Ghader anti-ship cruise missiles deployed in coastal batteries covering much of the Strait. Combined with coastal sensors, Iran can activate a multi-axis threat posture in the Strait. [Fact — Med, Army Recognition]


### B8.3) Maritime Security Belt 2026 (Joint Exercise — Russia, China, Iran)
* **Concurrent exercise:** "Maritime Security Belt 2026" — joint naval exercise held in the Strait of Hormuz concurrent with the IRGC unilateral drills. Russia, China, and Iran participating. Seventh iteration since 2019. [Fact — Tehran Times, TWZ]
* **Russian assets:** Russian Navy helicopter carrier docked in Iranian waters (Bandar Abbas) to participate. [Fact — Press TV]
* **Russian framing:** Nikolai Patrushev called for BRICS to be given "a full-fledged strategic maritime dimension" and cited the exercise as evidence. Russian naval commander said both sides are "ready to hold joint exercises in any region." [Fact — Tehran Times, Telegraph]
* **Operational implication:** Presence of Russian and Chinese warships in the Strait during any US attack on Iran would create military deconfliction requirements and political complications. Whether this is deliberate tripwire placement or routine exercise timing is ambiguous. [Inference — Med]

### B8.4) Assessment
The Hormuz exercise sequence represents a deliberate escalation in Iran's maritime deterrence signaling:

1. **Late Jan:** Live-fire exercise in Strait, no closure announcement.
2. **Feb 16:** Exercise restarts with missile firings from islands, drone/sub operations.
3. **Feb 17:** First-ever announced closure — crossing a rhetorical threshold Iran had long threatened but never executed.
4. **Concurrent:** Russia-China naval presence provides implicit escalation insurance.

The message is layered: to the US, that strikes will produce Hormuz disruption affecting global energy markets; to Gulf states, that their economies are hostage; to the international community, that Iran has partners willing to show presence during confrontation. The IRGC Navy commander's statement that "the weapon on the day of war is different" signals asymmetric reserves beyond what was displayed.

**Key analytical question:** Is this deterrence signaling (designed to prevent attack) or pre-positioning (preparation for retaliation)? The answer depends on whether the exercise included covert mine-laying or pre-positioning of fast attack boats in concealed positions — neither of which can be confirmed from open sources. [Inference — Med]

**Decision relevance for Scenario 2:** Any second US strike must now incorporate Hormuz disruption into planning. Oil price impact of even a temporary closure or mining threat could exceed the military cost of the strike itself. This strengthens the asymmetric deterrence calculus that makes a "clean" strike scenario implausible. [Inference — Med]
