### _L59) F1.3 US Mineral-Access Incentive Structure (New v1.2)
**Purpose:** Documents competing US interests that could fracture unified Western response to a post-deal crackdown scenario. Prevents ITB-F from treating the US (and Western governments broadly) as a monolithic actor on human rights.

**The competing pressure structure:**

US policy toward Iran is shaped by at minimum three competing institutional interests that pull in different directions on the question of condemnation:

1. **Nuclear security / nonproliferation track:** Dominant in stated policy. Drives deal-making and provides the public narrative. Represented by State Department, NSC, IAEA coordination.

2. **Human rights / democratic transition track:** Active in rhetoric, fragmented in institutional weight. Congressional pressure, diaspora advocacy, EU coordination. This is the track that would generate condemnation of a post-deal crackdown.

3. **Economic access / mineral track:** Not yet publicly visible in US negotiating framework, but structurally present. Iranian negotiators have explicitly activated it (Ghanbari offer, Feb 2026, Farsi sources). The US has documented critical mineral vulnerability (Frontier Baseline B12: 100% gallium import reliance, 90%+ of national defense stockpile depleted from Cold War peak, China export controls reimposable). Iranian copper, lithium, and strategic minerals represent a potential non-Chinese supply chain for energy transition materials. Private sector interests (mining companies, battery manufacturers, EV supply chain) have independent financial incentives that operate regardless of State Department positions.

**The institutional state of the US actor (Frontier Baseline, Pillar C):**

The current US executive operates on loyalty-first, transactional deal-making with minimal interagency process. The Iran negotiating team (Witkoff, Kushner) are real estate dealmakers, not strategic planners. The B12 vulnerability creates institutional pressure to close mineral supply chain gaps. An administration that has already demonstrated willingness to prioritize deal-making over procedural consistency would be particularly susceptible to a "minerals + nuclear" package that offers domestic economic wins alongside strategic compliance. A bilateral mining concession agreement requires no congressional action and no NSC formal review. [Inference — High, based on documented institutional structure]

**The historical template (documented):**

US condemned the 1973 Pinochet coup rhetorically. Within 12 months, the US was commercially normalizing with the junta. Chilean copper (25% of global supply) continued flowing to Western markets. Anaconda received $253M compensation for its nationalized Chilean assets. The human rights track produced official condemnation; the mineral-access track produced operational normalization. The two tracks ran simultaneously, with the mineral track determining actual policy outcomes.

**Implication for transition planning:**

Do not model US condemnation of a post-deal crackdown as guaranteed, durable, or action-generating. The mineral-access incentive creates a structural basis for at least some Western actors — private sector, energy transition interests, administration officials focused on supply chain independence — to calculate that a stable pro-Western authoritarian Iran provides greater long-term value than an unstable democratic transition. This calculation does not require coordination or conspiracy: it requires ordinary institutional self-interest operating in parallel with condemnation. The two tracks can run simultaneously, as they did in 1973-1974. [Inference — High]

**Monitoring trigger:** First English-language coverage explicitly linking Iranian mineral access to deal incentives. Currently documented only in Farsi sources (Ghanbari offer, Feb 2026). If/when it surfaces in English-language financial or trade press, the structural inference has become operational.

**Cross-reference:** ISA-TRAPS Trap 8 (condemnation assumption), ITB-A11 §A11.3.5, ITB-C §C2 (Sar Cheshmeh detail needed), Frontier Baseline B12 (critical mineral vulnerability), Frontier Baseline B24.2 (allied processing gap), Frontier Baseline A6.4 (Ghanbari offer documented in Farsi).

### _L292) F2.6 The Spoiler Mechanism — Formalized
**Mechanism type:** External deadline compression — Israel's strike threat forces Iran
to either (a) accept maximalist terms, (b) walk away, or (c) sign a deal before Israel acts,
after which Israel faces a choice between accepting fait accompli or striking regardless.

**How it differs from internal spoilers (A10):**
- A10 (eschatological faction) operates through institutional friction, delay, theological
  delegitimization — slow, grinding, works over months
- Israel operates through kinetic threat — can collapse the deal track in hours through a
  single military action

**Spoiler activation sequence:**
1. Deal terms emerge that Netanyahu assesses as insufficient (likely any deal that leaves
   enrichment infrastructure or proxy relationships intact)
2. Netanyahu escalates public demands + private Trump pressure + intelligence operations
   (satellite imagery presentations)
3. If Trump holds firm toward deal, Netanyahu faces choice: accept fait accompli or act
4. If acting, timing is before deal signing (post-signing action is far more costly
   politically) and while Iran remains degraded (reconstitution window)
5. US forced to respond: either back Israel (validate spoiler) or condemn Israel
   (rupture alliance publicly)

**The miscalculation pathway (Axios, Dec 31 2025):**
"Some US and Israeli officials think the likeliest spark for an Israel-Iran war in the
near-term is miscalculation — with one side trying to preempt a suspected attack by the other."
This is distinct from deliberate spoiler action — it can produce the same deal-foreclosing
outcome without Netanyahu making a calculated decision. [Fact — Axios]

#### F3.3.2 -- Port-Level Manifest Laundering (Sohar)

Sohar port functions as a physical manifest-laundering hub for Iranian-origin goods. The mechanism:

1. Goods (including petroleum products) arrive at Sohar from Iranian ports (e.g., Bandar Abbas).
2. Goods are unloaded, stored, and reloaded onto different vessels or transport.
3. Documentation is reissued identifying Sohar, Oman as the port of origin -- stripping the Iranian sanctions trace.
4. Goods enter destination markets (India, China, Africa, Gulf) as "Omani-origin."

**Verification:** The WSJ investigation into Adani Group (June 2025) documented this exact mechanism: an Iranian LPG shipment was handled via third-party logistics partners with documentation identifying Sohar, Oman, as the port of origin. Adani denied sanctions evasion, calling it a "routine commercial transaction." [Fact -- WSJ via Zawya, June 2025]

**Corroboration:** UANI has documented Iranian crude arriving in China "labeled as... Omani origin." [Fact -- UANI, cited in Wikipedia "Iranian shadow fleet"] The OFAC April 2025 maritime guidance specifically warns about falsified certificates of origin as a sanctions-evasion indicator. [Fact -- OFAC]

**Analytical note:** The re-export commission margin on this activity is not independently verified. Diaspora sources claim 10--20% intermediary commission; this is plausible given standard logistics arbitrage but cannot be treated as [Fact]. The *structural incentive* is confirmed: Oman earns port fees, logistics revenue, re-export commissions, and geopolitical leverage from this function. [Inference -- Med-High for mechanism; Low for specific commission rate]
