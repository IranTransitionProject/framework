**G6.4 Governance Precondition for Diaspora Capital Deployment**

Diaspora capital and professional repatriation are **exclusively Type B resources** (see ITB-F12 for full Type A/B framework). They activate ONLY under governance change — specifically requiring:

1. **Functioning property rights** — Under IRGC-dominant governance, any asset repatriated to Iran faces expropriation risk through no-bid contract displacement, regulatory extortion, or direct seizure. Iranian-Americans who have invested in Iran since 1979 have documented patterns of IRGC asset extraction. Capital does not return until this risk is structurally addressed (not just promised).

2. **IRGC-free banking destination** — Where does diaspora capital land? IRGC-penetrated banking system with OFAC-designated banks is unusable for diaspora investors with US/EU residency. Correspondent banking restoration requires IRGC divestiture from banking sector (see Trap 10, ISA-TRAPS).

3. **Personal security guarantees** — Returning Iranians with political profiles face detention, forced intelligence cooperation, or travel bans under current governance. 65% of Iran's engineering and science graduates plan to emigrate within 2 years of graduation (ISNA 2023). Brain drain reversal requires personal security guarantees that only governance change can structurally provide.

4. **Judicial contract enforcement** — No functioning commercial dispute resolution under IRGC-dominant Haqqani-pipeline judiciary (see ITB-D16). Foreign investors require enforceable contract rights. This is a governance problem, not a sanctions problem.

**Implication for transition planning:** All transition scenarios that rely on diaspora capital as a reconstruction resource must treat governance change (IRGC economic divestiture + banking reform + commercial law) as a precondition, not an aspiration. The current G6 framing treats diaspora as a ready-to-deploy resource. It is a conditionally ready resource — the condition is governance quality. Planning that assumes diaspora capital deploys under hollow deal will systematically overestimate reconstruction financing available in Scenario 1/1B.

**The February 14 signal:** The diaspora's political mobilization toward regime change (not deal-making) is correctly calibrated to their economic interests. This is not merely emotional opposition to the regime — it reflects a structural understanding that their capital and skills cannot deploy under IRGC-dominant governance. Diaspora political action is, in part, diaspora capital seeking its unlock conditions.

**Diaspora as Scenario 1B constraint:** Under hollow deal (Scenario 1B), diaspora becomes an active political constraint on deal durability in Western capitals. Iranian-American communities in Los Angeles (~350K mobilized Feb 14), Toronto (~350K), Munich (~250K) have demonstrated sustained political mobilization capacity. These communities have domestic political weight in US, Canadian, and German politics respectively. A deal that fails to include Type B conditionality will face sustained diaspora political opposition — making the deal politically costlier to maintain in Washington and Brussels over time. This is a resource for preventing hollow deal consolidation. [Inference–Med]
