## E1) Kurdish Regions — UPDATED
* **PJAK (PKK-affiliated):** Operates in border areas of Ilam and Kermanshah provinces. CTP-ISW reported (Jan 10, 2026) that Iranian authorities killed a group of PJAK fighters attempting to enter Iran from Iraq in Ilam and Kermanshah. [Fact — Med]
* **Kurdistan National Guard:** Emerged during Jan 2026 protests. Unclear if it has ties to PJAK or other Kurdish groups. [Uncertain — CTP-ISW]
* **KDPI:** Based in KRG Iraq. [Fact — Med]
* **Komala Party:** Publicly endorsed Dec 2025 protests and bazaari strikes; described them as legitimate responses to economic hardship. Held government responsible for worsening conditions. [Fact]
* **PKK disbandment:** PKK announced disbandment (context of Turkey normalization). Implications for PJAK unclear but may reduce cross-border militant capacity. [Fact — Med, implications uncertain]
* **Turkey gas pipeline:** Iran-Turkey gas pipeline contract expires July 2026. Turkey increasing domestic Black Sea gas production — reduced leverage for Iran, but also reduced reason for Turkey to destabilize Kurdish regions of Iran. [Fact — Med]
* **2022 precedent:** Kurdish communities in NW Iran were a major center of Mahsa Amini protests. Iranian leaders accused Kurdish groups in Iraqi Kurdistan of inciting unrest. [Fact — Med]
* **Most Iranian Kurds seek autonomy within Iran, not independence.** [Inference — Med]
* **Turkey views Kurdish autonomy as existential threat** — will oppose any transition outcome empowering Kurdish self-rule. [Fact — Med]
* **Transition risk: MEDIUM.** Kurdish regions most likely to experience localized armed activity during a power vacuum, but Iranian Kurdish movements do not have the military capacity or territorial control of Iraqi/Syrian Kurdish precedents. External exploitation by Turkey, KRG factions, or PJAK remnants is the primary concern. [Inference — Med]

## E2) Azerbaijani Northwest
* Largest minority, historically well-integrated. Pan-Turkic movements minority position. [Fact — Med]
* Khamenei himself is Azerbaijani — undermines separatist narrative but also means post-Khamenei landscape removes one integration mechanism. [Inference — Med]
* **Azerbaijan/Turkey factor:** Both have interests but neither has demonstrated intent to exploit Iranian Azerbaijani separatism. Turkey's priority is Kurdish containment. [Inference — Med]
* **Transition risk: LOW to MEDIUM** unless external actors inflame. [Inference — Med]

## E3) Balochistan — UPDATED
* Most underdeveloped region. Sunni. Sistan-Baluchestan province: 72% rainfall decline in 2024–25. [Fact — Med]
* **Jaish al-Adl:** Active Sunni insurgent group. Drug corridor. [Fact — Med]
* Cross-border with Pakistani Balochistan. Pakistan opposes Baloch empowerment. [Fact — Med]
* **IRGC checkpoint violence:** Summer 2025 — fatal shootings at IRGC/Basij checkpoints in multiple cities. Family including baby Raha killed at checkpoint (Jul 19, 2025). Pattern of post-war security measures being used as pretext for repression. [Fact]
* **Heightened paramilitary presence:** IRGC and Basij expanded operations in Baloch-majority regions — warrantless raids, home searches, mass detentions described as "preventative measures" against unrest. [Fact — Med]
* **Transition risk: MEDIUM** — most likely region to become ungoverned space during transition. Drug trafficking infrastructure would persist regardless of political arrangements. [Inference — Med]

## E4) Khuzestan — UPDATED
* Oil-rich. Arab minority. Environmentally stressed — worst-hit by water crisis and inter-basin transfers. [Fact — Med]
* **Water protests:** Farmers in Khuzestan explicitly protesting water rights, alleging IRGC-linked projects diverting water to central provinces. [Fact — Med, Geopolitical Monitor]
* **Environmental racism:** Scholars characterize water governance as environmental racism against Khuzestan Arabs, among others. [Inference — Med, academic sources]
* Gulf state interest. Limited separatist movements. [Fact — Med]
* **Transition risk: MEDIUM** — oil infrastructure makes it strategically critical. Water crisis is the most acute pressure point. Any transition must address Khuzestan water grievances early or risk losing Arab population support. [Inference — Med]

## E5) Assessment: Territorial Integrity Prognosis
* **Iran is NOT Iraq.** Iranian identity is ancient, deep, shared across ethnic boundaries. [Inference — High confidence in comparison]
* **Iran is NOT Libya.** Iran has bureaucratic state, national education system, shared culture. [Inference — High]
* **Stimson Center assessment (Feb 2026):** "If Iran unravels, it will do so unevenly and violently, resembling the fragmentation of Syria or Libya but on a far larger scale. Competing IRGC factions, provincial power centers, clerical networks, and armed non-state groups could battle for control." [Inference — Med, think tank]
* **Real risk is not ethnic fracture — it is:**
    1. Regional instability during security vacuum (Kurdish/Baloch exploitation)
    2. IRGC provincial fragmentation (32 regional divisions becoming independent actors)
    3. Water-driven center-periphery conflict (Khuzestan, Isfahan, SE Iran)
* **Refugee flows:** Stimson warns UAE (especially Dubai, with existing Iranian expatriate community) would be primary destination. Gulf states would face "complex security and administrative challenges, including vetting, immigration control, and the risk of infiltration." [Inference — Med]
