## A1) Constitutional Architecture (Level 0)
The Islamic Republic operates a dual-sovereignty system: theocratic authority (Supreme Leader, Guardian Council, Assembly of Experts) layered over republican institutions (President, Parliament, elected local councils). The theocratic layer holds veto power over the republican layer at every decision point. [Fact – High]

### _L21) A1.1 Supreme Leader's Office (Beit-e Rahbari)
- **Current holder:** Ayatollah Ali Khamenei (b. 1939, age 86). In office since June 1989. [Fact]
- **Constitutional powers:** Commander-in-chief of all armed forces; appoints heads of judiciary, state media (IRIB), and key military/security commanders; controls Expediency Council; approves or rejects presidential candidates (via Guardian Council); sets "general policies" of the state. [Fact – High]
- **Health status:** Persistent unconfirmed reports of declining health. No verified public medical disclosure. [Low – treat as [Uncertain]]
- **Succession mechanism:** Assembly of Experts (88 elected clerics) constitutionally responsible for selecting successor. In practice, IRGC influence over the process is widely assessed as decisive. [Inference – Med]
- **BOM Level 2 flag:** Supreme Leader succession is a single-point-of-failure for regime continuity. No tested transition mechanism exists – Khamenei is only the second holder of the office. The 1989 transition (Khomeini to Khamenei) occurred under radically different conditions (consolidated revolution, active war, charismatic founder). [Inference – Med]


### _L29) A1.2 Guardian Council
- **Composition:** 12 members – 6 clerics appointed by Supreme Leader; 6 jurists nominated by judiciary head (who is appointed by Supreme Leader) and approved by Parliament. Effective Supreme Leader control over all 12 seats. [Fact – High]
- **Function:** Vets all electoral candidates (presidential, parliamentary); reviews all parliamentary legislation for Islamic and constitutional compliance; can and does reject legislation and candidates. [Fact – High]
- **Transition relevance:** The Guardian Council is the primary mechanism by which the regime controls the boundaries of permissible political competition. Any transition that preserves elections but not the Guardian Council produces a fundamentally different system. Any transition that preserves the Guardian Council preserves regime control. [Inference – High]


### _L35) A1.3 Assembly of Experts
- **Composition:** 88 elected clerics, 8-year terms. Candidates vetted by Guardian Council. [Fact – High]
- **Function:** Constitutionally responsible for supervising and selecting/dismissing the Supreme Leader. In practice, has never exercised dismissal power and has functioned as a rubber stamp. [Fact – High]
- **Transition relevance:** Could theoretically serve as a transitional constitutional body if reformist faction gained control, but Guardian Council vetting prevents this. [Inference – Med]


### _L41) A1.4 Expediency Discernment Council
- **Function:** Mediates disputes between Parliament and Guardian Council; advises Supreme Leader. [Fact]
- **Transition relevance:** Low – advisory body with no independent enforcement capacity. [Inference]

## A2) Executive Branch
### _L50) A2.1 Presidency
- **Current holder:** Masoud Pezeshkian (inaugurated Jul 2024). [Fact – High]
- **Constitutional position:** Head of government for domestic administration. Subordinate to Supreme Leader on all security, military, and foreign policy matters. [Fact – High]
- **Current posture:** Pezeshkian was allowed to run as a "reformist" candidate. His administration has no independent power base capable of challenging IRGC or Supreme Leader authority on the protest response or nuclear negotiations. [Inference – Med]


### _L56) A2.2 Supreme National Security Council (SNSC)
- **Function:** Principal national security decision-making body. Coordinates between military, intelligence, and diplomatic apparatus. [Fact – High]
- **Chair:** The President (constitutionally), but Supreme Leader's representatives sit on it and hold effective veto. [Fact]
- **Transition relevance:** HIGH – the SNSC is where deal/no-deal decisions on the nuclear track are actually made. FM Araghchi negotiates; SNSC decides. [Inference – Med]
- **Internal contradiction signal:** SNSC deputy contradicted FM on enrichment offer – indicating either internal disagreement or deliberate good-cop/bad-cop diplomacy. [Fact – per Session Starter]

## A3) Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) – BOM FULL DECOMPOSITION REQUIRED
**This is the most important institutional section in the entire baseline. The IRGC is not merely a military force – it is a parallel state apparatus controlling military, economic, intelligence, and political functions simultaneously.**

### _L69) A3.1 IRGC Military (Level 1)
- **Ground Forces:** Conscript and volunteer force. Primary domestic repression instrument alongside Basij. [Fact]
- **Aerospace Force:** Missile program control. Ballistic missile inventory (Shahab, Khorramshahr, Emad series). Drone program. Air defense. [Fact]
  - **Khorramshahr-4:** Deployed in underground facilities – shift from defensive to offensive posture. [Fact – per A2 update]
  - **Chinese YLC-8B:** Anti-stealth radar deployed. [Fact – per A2 update]
- **Navy (IRGCN):** Asymmetric naval force – fast attack craft, mines, coastal defense missiles. Controls Persian Gulf/Strait of Hormuz operations. [Fact]
  - **Feb 3, 2026:** IRGC gunboats attempted to seize US tanker; Iranian drone shot down approaching USS Abraham Lincoln. [Fact – per A2 update]
  - **IRIS Shahid Bagheri:** Naval drone carrier photographed near Bandar Abbas Jan 27 and Feb 10. [Fact – per A2 update, satellite imagery]
- **Quds Force (IRGC-QF):** External operations – proxy management, foreign intelligence, extraterritorial military operations. [Fact]
  - **Post-Soleimani status:** Reorganized under Esmail Qaani (succeeded Soleimani 2020). Operational effectiveness degraded after 2024 Hezbollah decimation and Israel-Iran direct conflict. [Inference – Med]
  - **Current proxy network status:** [GAP – requires dedicated research. See Appendix C.]


### _L82) A3.2 IRGC Intelligence Organization (Level 1)
- Parallel intelligence service to MOIS. Focused on internal security threats, ideological enforcement, and counter-espionage. [Fact – Med]
- Historically rivals MOIS for primacy – rivalry is itself a regime stability mechanism (divide and monitor). [Inference – Med]
- **Transition relevance:** Any security transition must account for TWO parallel intelligence services with overlapping but competing capabilities and loyalties.


### _L88) A3.3 IRGC Economic Empire (Level 1) – BOM
- **Scope:** Estimated 25-40% of Iranian GDP flows through IRGC-controlled entities. [Inference – Low/Med – estimates vary widely]
- **Sectors:** Construction (Khatam al-Anbiya – largest contractor in Iran; also served as dual-use procurement channel for military underground construction – see C2), telecommunications, oil and gas, banking, import/export, smuggling networks, bonyads (religious foundations turned conglomerates). [Fact – Med]
- **Mechanism:** IRGC entities receive preferential contracts, sanctions-evasion channels, and regulatory exemptions. This creates an economic constituency for regime preservation that is independent of ideological loyalty. [Inference – Med]
- **BOM Level 2 flag:** The IRGC economic empire is the single largest simultaneity trap in Iran transition planning. Dismantling it too fast collapses employment and economic output. Preserving it perpetuates the parallel state. This is the Iran equivalent of the bootstrapping problem. [Inference – see ISA]


### _L95) A3.4 Basij Resistance Force (Level 1)
- **Organization:** Volunteer paramilitary under IRGC. Estimated 1-5 million mobilizable personnel (wide range reflects uncertainty about active vs. nominal membership). [Inference – Low]
- **Function:** Internal security, morality enforcement, protest suppression, ideological mobilization. [Fact]
- **Transition relevance:** Basij members are embedded in every neighborhood, university, and workplace. They are the regime's distributed surveillance and enforcement network. Transition planning must account for what happens to this population – demobilization, reintegration, or continued resistance. [Inference – Med]

## A4) Regular Armed Forces (Artesh)
- **Distinct from IRGC:** The Artesh is the conventional military – army, navy, air force. Constitutionally separate from the IRGC. [Fact]
- **Loyalty dynamics:** Artesh is generally assessed as less ideologically committed to the regime than IRGC. Officers come from a broader social base. Historical precedent (1979) shows conventional military can fracture during revolutionary moments. [Inference – Med]
- **BOM flag:** The Artesh-IRGC relationship is a critical variable in any transition scenario. If Artesh units refuse orders or side with protesters, regime security capacity degrades rapidly. If Artesh remains loyal, the security apparatus has depth the IRGC alone does not. [Inference – Med]

## A5) Judiciary
- **Head:** Appointed by Supreme Leader. [Fact]
- **Function:** Prosecution of dissidents, enforcement of Islamic law, property disputes, commercial law. [Fact]
- **Transition relevance:** The judiciary is both a repression instrument and a necessary civil institution. Wholesale replacement creates legal vacuum; preservation perpetuates injustice. Hybrid approaches (lustration + institutional reform) are the historical model. [Inference – Med]
