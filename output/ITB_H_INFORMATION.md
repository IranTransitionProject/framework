### H1.2) Technical Characterization
* **Most severe in Iranian history.** Georgia Tech: "most sophisticated and most severe in Iran's history." [Fact]
* **Unprecedented NIN disruption:** Unlike 2019 or 2022, even the domestic intranet was initially disconnected. This was noted as rushed implementation — appeared unplanned. [Fact — Med, Project Ainita]
* **Shift from blacklist to whitelist:** Previous approach: block specific sites/apps (blacklisting). New approach: only allow government-approved sites/services (whitelisting). Fundamental shift. [Fact — CNN, Chatham House]
* **~3% connectivity persisted** during full blackout — likely government officials and state services. [Fact — Georgia Tech]
* **Jagged restoration:** Since partial restoration, traffic patterns "very jagged" — Kentik speculates new filtering system installed that can't keep up. [Fact — Med]
* **VPN disruption:** Sophisticated systems identifying and blocking VPN traffic. Proton VPN user base in Iran cut off. [Fact]
* **Starlink countermeasures:** GPS jamming to disrupt Starlink access. 30% packet loss average, 80% in some areas. [Fact — Miaan Group]


### H1.3) "Barracks Internet" Architecture — CRITICAL FINDING
* **Confidential planning documents (obtained by Filterwatch):** Regime developing "Barracks Internet" — permanent two-tier system where global web access granted only through strict security whitelist. [Fact — Med, Rest of World]
* **Government spokesperson Mohajerani:** International access "will not be restored until at least late March." Filterwatch sources indicate access will "never return to its previous form." [Fact]
* **Supreme Court demand:** National Information Network to be deployed immediately. [Fact]
* **Comparison:** North Korea built intranet for population that never had connectivity. China built Great Firewall over two decades with domestic alternatives. Iran trying to do both in weeks with NO domestic alternatives. [Inference — Med, Chatham House]
* **Experts' assessment:** "No country has hermetically sealed a functioning digital economy." Communications Minister Hashemi himself called the hardliner demand to move fully to domestic internet a "bitter joke" that is "not feasible." [Fact]
* **TRANSITION RELEVANCE:** If Barracks Internet is implemented, ANY transition inherits a bifurcated digital infrastructure. Restoring full connectivity would be both technically and politically complex — and an early deliverable that citizens would demand immediately. [Inference — Med]

## H2) Economic Impact of Blackout — UPDATED
* **Official cost:** ICT Minister Hashemi: at least 50 trillion rials (~$33M) daily — admitted true toll "likely much higher." [Fact]
* **NetBlocks estimate:** Exceeds $37M/day. [Fact]
* **Online sales:** Fell 80% during shutdown. [Fact]
* **Tehran Stock Exchange:** Lost 450,000 points over 4 days. 130 trillion tomans daily losses. [Fact]
* **Financial transactions:** Dropped by 185 million in January 2026. [Fact]
* **10M+ Iranians** depend directly on digital platforms for livelihoods. [Fact — Med]
* **Foreign trade disrupted:** Import/export processes "heavily dependent on internet from initial stages" — price negotiations, invoices, transportation coordination, document verification all blocked. [Fact — Al Jazeera/Chamber of Commerce]
* **Food supply chain:** Countries unwilling to tie food security to unstable supply conditions. Customer attrition, especially in food commodities. [Fact — Al Jazeera]
* **Hashemi's 20-day estimate:** Ministry estimated online businesses could survive ~20 days under blackout — implying state knew forced restoration was necessary. [Fact]

## H3) Psychological Impact
* **Mass trauma:** Mental health experts describe blackouts as intensifying anxiety, isolation, and trauma in already-fragile society. [Fact — Iran International]
* **"Anticipatory anxiety":** Even after partial restoration, society remains on edge — fear of being cut off again. [Fact — Khabar Online]
* **Information overload cycle:** Days/weeks of isolation → sudden exposure to graphic images → panic attacks, dissociation, intrusive thoughts, PTSD symptoms, suicide risk. "Cumulative, long-term harm." [Fact — psychologist assessment]
* **PTSD from blackout-as-weapon:** The blackout itself is traumatic — disconnection from family, friends, news, livelihood, world. Distinct from the violence it conceals. [Inference — Med]

### H4.1) External Information Operations Targeting Iranian Population — NEW, Feb 24
**50,000-person text message campaign:** Approximately 50,000 Iranians received a text message reading: "To the oppressed people of Iran: the US president is a man of action, you will soon see." [Fact -- CBS News, NCRI]

**Assessment:** This represents a direct psychological operations outreach to Iranian civilians during the blackout period, delivered via SMS (which was restored Jan 17). The message targets the population's hope for external intervention while the regime's internet blackout prevents counter-messaging through normal digital channels. Attribution unclear from open sources — could be US government, diaspora organization, or third-party actor. The scale (50K) suggests organized capability, not individual effort. [Inference -- Med]

**IRGC intelligence chief internet confirmation:** IRGC intelligence chief Majid Khademi confirmed that Khamenei personally tasked him with national internet expansion/control. This is the first confirmed direct Supreme Leader directive linking IRGC intelligence (not ICT Ministry) to internet infrastructure control. [Fact -- CNN]

**Implication:** Confirms that internet control is a security function under IRGC intelligence, not a civilian ICT function. The "Barracks Internet" architecture (H1.3) is being built under IRGC, not civilian, authority. Any transition scenario must account for the fact that restoring internet access requires displacing IRGC intelligence control of the infrastructure, not merely changing ICT Ministry policy. [Inference -- Med-High]

## H7) Information Environment Assessment
* **Current state:** Iran is in the most severe information suppression phase in its history. The shift from blackout → Barracks Internet represents a qualitative change — not temporary crisis response but attempted permanent digital isolation.
* **Regime dilemma:** Cannot maintain digital isolation without crippling economy. Communications Minister himself acknowledges infeasibility. But cannot restore full access without losing information control that enabled massacre cover-up.
* **Opposition advantage:** Diaspora information infrastructure (Psiphon, Starlink, satellite hacking, border documentation) has proven remarkably resilient. Even under total blackout, information leaked out.
* **Transition implication:** Information restoration would be one of the first demands of any post-regime population. The technical infrastructure exists — the regime's own equipment works fine — but the surveillance architecture layered on top creates a transition engineering challenge. [Inference — Med]
