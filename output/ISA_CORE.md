## 3) CASCADING FAILURE INDEX (CFI)
**Purpose:** Identify "Master Nodes" -- components that, if severed, trigger failures across 3+ pillars simultaneously.

### _L95) A. Supreme Leader Death/Incapacitation
* **Mechanism:** Supreme Leader is the constitutional apex of both theocratic and military hierarchies. Loss severs the command authority that holds IRGC, judiciary, Guardian Council, and state media under unified control.
* **Impacted Pillars:** A (institutional collapse), B (military command fragmentation), C (economic patronage networks lose coordinator), E (regional actors test boundaries), F (international stakeholders recalculate), H (narrative vacuum).
* **CFI Score:** **6 pillars impacted.**
* **ITB anchors:** A1.1, A3, A7. [Inference -- Med]


### _L102) B. IRGC Factional Split
* **Mechanism:** IRGC is not monolithic. Factions exist (hardliner vs. pragmatist, military vs. economic, generational). Under succession stress or deal-making pressure, IRGC could fragment into competing armed groups -- each controlling economic assets, weapons, and territory.
* **Impacted Pillars:** A (regime structure collapses), B (security vacuum + armed faction warfare), C (IRGC economic empire fragments; asset-stripping), E (regional IRGC commanders become warlords), G (diaspora return becomes impossible).
* **CFI Score:** **5 pillars impacted.**
* **[HISTORICAL PARALLEL: Libya]** -- Libyan military fragmentation post-Gaddafi is the closest analog. Difference: Iran's IRGC is more institutionally structured than Libyan militias, which cuts both ways (harder to fragment, but fragments are more capable if it does).
* **ITB anchors:** A3, A1.1. [Inference -- Med]


### _L110) C. Oil Infrastructure Disruption (Khuzestan)
* **Mechanism:** Iran's oil export infrastructure is concentrated in Khuzestan province -- the same province with Arab minority grievances and Gulf state interests. Conflict, sabotage, or separatist action targeting Khuzestan oil facilities severs the primary revenue source for any government (current or successor).
* **Impacted Pillars:** C (revenue collapse), B (military must defend infrastructure), E (territorial integrity tested), F (oil market disruption triggers international intervention calculus).
* **CFI Score:** **4 pillars impacted.**
* **[HISTORICAL PARALLEL: Iraq]** -- Post-2003 oil infrastructure was a primary insurgent/terrorist target; control of oil was central to ISIS revenue model.
* **ITB anchors:** C1, E4. [Inference -- Med]


### _L118) D. Internet/Communications Infrastructure
* **Mechanism:** Regime controls centralized internet kill switch. Chinese surveillance tech is embedded. In blackout conditions, no internal coordination is possible for either protesters or transition planners. Information vacuum prevents assessment of ground truth.
* **Impacted Pillars:** H (narrative vacuum), D (protest coordination severed), G (diaspora cannot communicate with internal population), B (security forces operate without public accountability).
* **CFI Score:** **4 pillars impacted.**
* **ITB anchors:** B4, H1. [Fact/Inference -- High]


### _L125) E. Nuclear Facility Security Lapse
* **Mechanism:** During transition chaos, nuclear facilities (even post-Midnight Hammer damaged ones) and remaining nuclear material require security. Loss of unified command over nuclear security triggers international intervention regardless of other transition dynamics.
* **Impacted Pillars:** B (security), F (international -- IAEA, US, Israel all have red lines), A (whoever controls nuclear material has regime leverage).
* **CFI Score:** **3+ pillars impacted.**
* **ITB anchors:** B1, F9. [Inference -- High]

## 5) INTERVENTION SEQUENCING
**Purpose:** Establish that transition interventions cannot be executed in arbitrary order. Same Phase 1/2/3 logic as the Frontier project SSA, adapted for transition context.

### _L138) Phase 1: Buffer (Immediate -- Days to Months)
*Requires only political will and international cooperation. No prior capability needed.*

| Priority | Intervention | Mechanism | Prerequisite |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **Ceasefire / Security Stabilization** | Stop active killing. Artesh maintains order; IRGC combat units confined. International monitors if possible. | Political agreement (internal or imposed) |
| 2 | **Communications Restoration** | Lift internet blackout. Enable information flow. Essential for all subsequent coordination. | Regime compliance or technical bypass |
| 3 | **Humanitarian Corridor** | Emergency food, medicine, medical care. Address immediate casualties and deprivation. | International logistics + access |
| 4 | **Nuclear Facility Security** | IAEA access and international monitoring of all nuclear sites. Non-negotiable for international support. | Agreement with whatever authority controls sites |
| 5 | **Transitional Authority Declaration** | Some recognized body (interim government, national council, or equivalent) that can serve as international interlocutor. | Internal political consensus (hardest step) |


### _L149) Phase 2: Rebuild (Months to Years)
*Requires Phase 1 outputs as prerequisites.*

| Priority | Intervention | Mechanism | Phase 1 Prerequisite |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 6 | **Constitutional Process** | Elected constituent assembly drafts new constitution. Guardian Council abolished or reformed. | Transitional authority (#5) + communications (#2) + security (#1) |
| 7 | **Sanctions Relief (Phased)** | Conditional on nuclear compliance, human rights benchmarks, constitutional process milestones. | Nuclear security (#4) + international interlocutor (#5) |
| 8 | **IRGC Economic Transition** | Freeze assets under transitional authority. Establish parallel economic channels. Begin phased privatization/dissolution. | Sanctions relief (#7) + security (#1) + transitional authority (#5) |
| 8b | **Parallel Society Economic Absorption** | Create alternative employment pathways for IRGC-dependent population before institutional dissolution. Open compound infrastructure to public use. Individual-level vetting, not blanket exclusion. | Sanctions relief (#7) + transitional authority (#5) + security (#1) |
| 9 | **Security Sector Reform** | Artesh professionalization. IRGC combat unit dissolution/integration. Basij demobilization with vetting. | Security stabilization (#1) + constitutional authority (#6) |
| 10 | **Judiciary Reform** | Lustration + institutional reform. Hybrid approach: remove most compromised; retrain remainder. | Constitutional authority (#6) |


### _L161) Phase 3: Structural (Years to Decades)
*Requires Phase 2 outputs. Cannot be shortcut.*

| Priority | Intervention | Mechanism | Phase 2 Prerequisite |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 11 | **Democratic Elections** | First free elections under new constitution. | Constitution (#6) + security (#9) |
| 12 | **Economic Diversification** | Reduce oil dependency. Develop non-hydrocarbon sectors. Leverage diaspora investment. | Sanctions relief (#7) + IRGC economic transition (#8) |
